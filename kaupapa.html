Kaupapa

Five areas of mahi — different paths, one purpose.

My mahi doesn't fit neatly into one box. It spans research, clinical care, natural health, governance, and community — and that's intentional. Each area is a different expression of the same kaupapa: improving health outcomes for whānau Māori by working at multiple levels at once.

These five pou are the pillars of that work. They're connected. They inform each other. And they all come back to the same question: how does this support pae ora for our people?

Pou 01

PhD Research — Understanding what works

Exploring Indigenous models of diabetes care to identify the values and components that support real, sustained hauora. Not just what works clinically — but what works for whānau. Grounded in kaupapa Māori methodology and community partnership.

Read more →
Pou 02

Kia Kaha Chemist

A mobile and online pharmacy service focused on meeting people where they are — in their homes, their communities, and their realities. Because access, trust, and understanding matter just as much as the medicine itself.

Read more →
Pou 03

Natural Health — Supporting the body differently

Developing natural health products grounded in lived experience and emerging science — through The Brothers Green and Good Sans. Creating practical tools that support stress, recovery, and everyday wellbeing in ways that fit into how people actually live.

Read more →
Pou 04

Governance — Shaping the conditions

Working across governance roles to influence how systems are designed, funded, and delivered. Because hauora is shaped long before someone walks into a clinic — and the decisions made at governance level matter enormously.

Read more →
Pou 05

Community — Living the kaupapa

Co-chair of a local kōhanga reo, supporting the growth of our tamariki and whānau through language, culture, and connection. Because hauora starts with identity, belonging, and whānau — and that work happens in community.
