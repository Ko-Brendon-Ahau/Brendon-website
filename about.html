Ko Brendon ahau

I'm a pharmacist and researcher based in Aotearoa New Zealand. My mahi is centred on pursuing pae ora for whānau Māori, across clinical practice, research, natural health, and contributions to community and health system spaces.

This is an ongoing journey where I'm learning, working alongside whānau, and contributing where I can. I believe meaningful change happens when we work across multiple levels at once, from individual care through to system change.

Background

I am a Kāi Tahu pharmacist with a Master of Health Science, currently undertaking a PhD focused on developing a medicine review process for whānau living with type 2 diabetes.

Much of my thinking has been shaped by working directly with whānau in community settings. Through Kia Kaha Chemist, I began going into homes, marae, and local spaces; sitting alongside whānau and having real conversations about their medicines and their health.

What I saw early on stayed with me.

Whānau choosing between kai and prescriptions. Kaumātua taking multiple medicines without fully understanding what they were for. People experiencing side effects, frustration, and uncertainty, often without feeling comfortable enough to ask questions in clinical settings.

It made it clear that the issue wasn't just medicines, it was access, understanding, and connection.

Those experiences continue to shape both my research and my practice. They've reinforced the importance of meeting whānau where they are, taking the time to explain, and ensuring people have the knowledge to make informed decisions about their health.

Alongside this, I have completed postgraduate training in Nutritional and Environmental Medicine, which supports a more holistic view of hauora, recognising the role of nutrition, lifestyle, and environment alongside medicines.

How I work

Over time, my approach has evolved beyond a traditional pharmacy model.

My work now sits at the intersection of:

  • medicines and clinical care
  • health education and literacy
  • nutrition and natural health approaches
  • system-level thinking about how services are designed

I'm particularly interested in how we can strengthen the foundations of health, supporting whānau to better understand their tinana, their medicines, and their choices, while still using medicines appropriately where they are needed.

This also extends into exploring natural health solutions, including hemp-based supplements, Rongoā rākau and other botanicals, with a focus on accessibility, regeneration and supporting whānau-led approaches to hauora.

Values

The way I approach this mahi is guided by:

Tino rangatiratanga — supporting Māori self-determination in health

Whānau-centred care — valuing the collective as the foundation of wellbeing

Equity — of care and therefore outcomes

Cultural integrity — ensuring care aligns with Māori values and ways of knowing

The kaupapa

Everything I do connects back to one kaupapa: improving health outcomes for our people.

Whether I'm working alongside whānau, contributing to research, developing products, or involved in service and system-level discussions, I come back to a simple question:

How does this support more equitable health outcomes for whānau Māori?

That pātai continues to guide the path I'm on.
