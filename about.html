Ko Brendon Ahau

I’m a pharmacist and researcher based in Aotearoa New Zealand. My mahi is centred on supporting better health outcomes for Māori, through a combination of clinical practice, research, natural health, and involvement in community and health system spaces. This is an ongoing journey, learning, working alongside whānau, and contributing where I can.

My mahi is driven by a single purpose: improving health outcomes for whānau Māori. I pursue that through several different paths; research, clinical health services, natural health products, and community governance, because I believe meaningful change requires working at multiple levels at once.

Background

I am a Māori pharmacist with a Master of Health Science, currently undertaking a PhD focused on developing a medicine review process for whānau living with type 2 diabetes. Alongside my research, I work directly with whānau through community-based pharmacy services; meeting whānau where they are, whether that’s in their whare, on marae, or in local spaces. These experiences continue to shape how I understand health and wellbeing. I have also completed postgraduate training in Nutritional and Environmental Medicine, which supports a more holistic approach to hauora; recognising the role of lifestyle, environment, and broader determinants of health. Through different roles and contributions, I aim to support work that strengthens health services and systems in ways that are more responsive to whānau Māori.

Values

The way I approach this mahi is guided by:

Tino rakatirataka — supporting Māori self-determination in health

Whānau-centred care — valuing the collective as the foundation of wellbeing

Equity — of care and therefore outcomes

Cultural integrity — ensuring care aligns with Māori values and ways of knowing

The kaupapa

Everything I do connects back to one kaupapa: improving health outcomes for our people.

Whether I’m working alongside whānau, contributing to research, or involved in service and system-level discussions, I try to come back to a simple question:

How does this support better outcomes for Māori?

That question continues to guide the path I’m on.
