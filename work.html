Five pillars

Mahi

Different paths, one purpose: pae ora for whānau Māori

My mahi spans five interconnected areas. They each operate differently, but they all serve the same kaupapa: improving health outcomes for Māori communities in Aotearoa — from individual care through to system change.

🔬 Rakahau/Research

My research work includes my PhD and other collaborative projects. The focus is on generating evidence that directly serves Māori health equity — research that is community-led, culturally grounded, and practically useful.

Read more about my research →

💊 Clinical Health Services

Kia Kaha Chemist

Kia Kaha Chemist is a pharmacy, medicine and supplement review service built around whānau. It's about making quality health services accessible, culturally safe, and community-centred, meeting people where they are, whether that's online, in their whare, on marae, or in local spaces.

Visit kiakahachemist.org.nz →

🌿 Natural Health Products

The Brothers Green

The Brothers Green develops natural health products grounded in Māori values and traditional knowledge. The goal is to make high-quality natural health accessible to our communities, with a focus on hemp-based supplements and Rongoā-informed approaches.

Visit thebrothersgreen.co →

Good Sans

Good Sans is a new project currently in development, focused on natural health solutions for the sandwich generation.

Website coming soon.

🏛️ Governance & Leadership

I sit on several boards and am involved in community organisations working toward systemic change in Māori health and wellbeing. This work is about influencing the structures and systems that shape health outcomes at scale.
