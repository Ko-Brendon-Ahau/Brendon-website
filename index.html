On hauora, systems, and where health really begins

Understanding hauora — and the systems around it — in a way that actually makes sense.

Kia ora, ko Brendon ahau

Pharmacist and PhD researcher exploring Indigenous models of diabetes care. My mahi spans governance, community, clinical care, and natural health — all connected by one kaupapa: making sense of hauora in a way that connects to how we actually live.

Ngā pou e rima

Five areas of mahi

Different expressions of the same kaupapa — each one a pillar, each one connected.

Pou 01

PhD Research — Understanding what works

Exploring Indigenous models of diabetes care to identify the values and components that support real, sustained hauora. Not just what works clinically — but what works for whānau.

Read more →
Pou 02

Kia Kaha Chemist

A mobile and online pharmacy service focused on meeting people where they are — in their homes, their communities, and their realities. Because access, trust, and understanding matter just as much as the medicine itself.

Read more →
Pou 03

Natural Health — Supporting the body differently

Developing natural health products grounded in lived experience and emerging science. Creating practical tools that support stress, recovery, and everyday wellbeing — in ways that fit into how people actually live.

Read more →
Pou 04

Governance — Shaping the conditions

Working across governance roles to influence how systems are designed, funded, and delivered. Because hauora is shaped long before someone walks into a clinic.

Read more →
Pou 05

Community — Living the kaupapa

Co-chair of a local kōhanga reo, supporting the growth of our tamariki and whānau through language, culture, and connection. Because hauora starts with identity, belonging, and whānau.

Read more →

Health is shaped by systems, environments, and knowledge. When those align, people thrive.

When they don't, the work is about changing conditions — not blaming individuals.
