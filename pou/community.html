Pou 05 — Hāpori

Living the kaupapa

Hauora starts with identity, belonging, and whānau.

Kōhanga reo

I am co-chair of a local kōhanga reo — a Māori language immersion early childhood centre. This role sits at the heart of what I believe about hauora: that health starts with identity, language, and belonging.

Kōhanga reo are more than language nests. They're places where tamariki grow up knowing who they are, where they come from, and that they belong. That foundation — of identity and connection — is one of the most powerful determinants of long-term wellbeing.

Why community matters

All the clinical work, research, and governance in the world won't shift health outcomes if the foundations aren't there. Community is where those foundations are built — through relationships, through culture, through the everyday acts of showing up for each other.

This pou is a reminder that the kaupapa isn't just something I work on. It's something I live.
